## 1. Current State Summary

Blooming Marvellous is a boutique florist on the Costa Blanca South (Spain), based at
Urbanización Oasis, La Marina (Alicante province). The owner (Lorraine) described the presence as
minimal. Research surfaced a thin existing footprint: an old website
(`bloomingmarvellouslamarina.com`) **managed by a third party who no longer builds sites and is
being left behind**, an Instagram (`@blooming_marvellous_florist`) and a Facebook page
(`/bloomingmarvellouslamarina`, ~423 likes) carrying genuine positive reviews. This is a clean
from-scratch build on the **new** domain: the new site will be the brand's first proper,
content-rich digital home.

- **Existing website**: `bloomingmarvellouslamarina.com` (present but no extractable content)
- **Social channels covered**: Instagram `@blooming_marvellous_florist`, Facebook `/bloomingmarvellouslamarina`
- **Google Business Profile**: not confirmed
- **Overall digital maturity**: LOW

### Observations

- Local, owner-led boutique florist serving expat-heavy urbanisations along the southern
  Costa Blanca (La Marina, San Fulgencio, Guardamar, Ciudad Quesada, Rojales, Torrevieja,
  Santa Pola, Elche, Alicante).
- USP is bespoke, made-to-order arrangements with a "special personal touch" plus
  **free local delivery** in and around La Marina — a genuinely local promise the big
  delivery platforms can't credibly make.
- Multilingual market reality: the catchment is mixed English / Spanish (and Brisalta, a
  direct local peer, also offers Russian). English-first copy is the right primary, with
  Spanish a strong future add.

### Weaknesses

- Zero discoverability today — no site, so all custom currently comes from word-of-mouth or
  passing trade.
- No online showcase of arrangement quality, which is the single most persuasive asset a
  florist has.

### Opportunities

- Own "local + personal + free delivery" — the exact gap the national platforms leave open.
- Lead with photography of real bespoke work to justify the soft-luxury positioning.
- Make enquiry frictionless (WhatsApp / phone / form) rather than forcing a full ecommerce
  checkout the business doesn't need yet.

### Patterns across the category

- **Occasion-led navigation** is universal (Birthdays, Love/Romance, Anniversary, New Baby,
  Sympathy/Funerals, Weddings, Congratulations, Thank You).
- **Same-day / fast delivery** is the headline trust signal everywhere.
- **"Real photo before delivery"** and **fresh-daily** are recurring reassurances.
- Local peers (Brisalta) convert via **WhatsApp / phone enquiry + a tiered price-band
  catalogue**, not a full shopping cart — the right pattern for a boutique.
- Premium florists lean on **emotional, romantic tone** and **large photography**.

## 4. Opportunities to Outperform

- **Opportunity 1**: Win on genuine localness — name the actual urbanisations served and
  promise free local delivery up front.
  - _Grounded in_: §1 USP; the national platforms (§3) can't make a credible local promise.
- **Opportunity 2**: Sell the bespoke craft visually — a gallery of real arrangements and an
  occasions grid, instead of a generic product catalogue.
  - _Grounded in_: soft-luxury brand direction; Brisalta's tiered showcase (§3) proves the model.
- **Opportunity 3**: Frictionless, human ordering — WhatsApp + phone + a short enquiry form,
  matching how a boutique actually takes orders.
  - _Grounded in_: Brisalta's WhatsApp-led UX (§3); avoids over-building ecommerce the business
    doesn't need yet.

## 5. Content Direction

- **Tone of voice**: Warm, romantic, personal, artisan — "premium without being exclusive."
  Editorial and spacious, never salesy.
- **Reading level**: Easy, conversational English. Short sentences.
- **Core topics / service framing**: Bespoke arrangements for every occasion; weddings &
  events; sympathy & funeral tributes; free local delivery; the personal touch.
- **Trust signals to prioritise**: Free local delivery, same-day for local orders, made by
  hand to order, fresh flowers, real local florist (not a faceless platform).
- **Hero headline direction**: Lead with the brand essence + the personal, local promise.
- **Primary CTA direction**: "Order / enquire" routed to WhatsApp or phone; secondary CTA to
  browse the gallery or occasions.

## 6. Differentiation Strategy

**One-line differentiator**: A local Costa Blanca florist hand-making bespoke arrangements for
every occasion, with that special personal touch and free local delivery — the warmth of a
neighbourhood florist with boutique polish.

### Proof points

1. Bespoke, made-to-order arrangements (not off-the-shelf bouquets).
2. Free delivery in and around La Marina, San Fulgencio and the surrounding Costa Blanca South.
3. A real local florist with a personal touch — not a national delivery platform.

### Messaging architecture

- **Headline idea**: "Flowers for every moment that matters."
- **Sub-headline idea**: Bespoke arrangements with a personal touch, hand-made on the Costa
  Blanca and delivered free to your door.
- **Three-word brand essence**: Bespoke · Personal · Local
